Climate & Ecological Modelling

Introduction

I work at the intersection of climate science and ecology, focusing on how environmental change shapes animal systems. My work combines climate data, ecological knowledge, and modelling to build clear and defensible analyses.

I am particularly interested in migratory systems, where large-scale atmospheric processes interact with behavioural and ecological constraints.

What I do

  • Develop data-driven models linking climate and ecology.
  • Analyse large environmental datasets, including climate, satellite, and tracking data.
  • Translate ecological questions into quantitative frameworks.
  • Evaluate assumptions, uncertainty, and model behaviour.

Selected projects

Flyway model

A model that simulates seabird flyways using wind, distance, and food availability, then compares the results with real tracking data.

Arctic climate & seasonal timing

Analysis of how warming, snow cover, and rainfall extremes are changing the reproductive window for Arctic-breeding birds.

Vegetation timing and nutrient peaks

Satellite and climate-data analysis used to estimate when Arctic vegetation reaches key growth stages and how that may change in the future.

Arctic tern wind strategies

A comparison of Dutch and Svalbard Arctic terns to understand how different populations respond to wind along the Atlantic flyway.
